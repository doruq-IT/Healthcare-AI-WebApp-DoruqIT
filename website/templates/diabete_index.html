Diabetes Risk Analysis

Overview

Diabetes mellitus encompasses a variety of diseases that impact the way your body processes blood sugar (glucose), which is crucial for health. Glucose serves as a key energy source for muscle and tissue cells and is the primary fuel for the brain. The root causes of diabetes differ depending on its type. Regardless of the type, diabetes results in elevated blood sugar levels, which can pose significant health risks if not managed properly. Chronic forms of diabetes include type 1 and type 2 diabetes. There are also forms of diabetes that are potentially reversible, such as prediabetes and gestational diabetes. Prediabetes is a condition where blood sugar levels are above normal but not high enough to be deemed diabetes, and it can often lead to diabetes if no preventive steps are taken. Gestational diabetes occurs during pregnancy and typically resolves after childbirth.

Symptoms

The symptoms of diabetes can differ based on the level of blood sugar elevation. It's common for individuals, particularly those with prediabetes or type 2 diabetes, to not exhibit symptoms at times. In contrast, symptoms in type 1 diabetes usually develop rapidly and are often more intense. Common indicators for both type 1 and type 2 diabetes include:

  • Experiencing heightened thirst and a significant increase in hunger
  • Needing to urinate more frequently than usual
  • Weight loss that cannot be explained
  • Detection of ketones in urine (ketones are produced when muscle and fat are broken down due to insufficient insulin)
  • Feeling unusually tired
  • Experiencing irritability
  • Having blurred vision
