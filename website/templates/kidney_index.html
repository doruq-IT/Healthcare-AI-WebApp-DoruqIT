Kidney Health Evaluation

Overview

Chronic kidney disease, often referred to as chronic renal failure, is characterized by a gradual decline in kidney functionality. The primary role of your kidneys is to filter out waste and excess fluids from your blood, which are then eliminated through urine. Advanced chronic kidney disease can lead to the accumulation of dangerous levels of fluids, electrolytes, and waste in your body. In its initial stages, chronic kidney disease may not exhibit many symptoms. Often, the disease only becomes noticeable when there is a considerable decline in kidney function. The management of chronic kidney disease primarily aims at slowing down the progression of kidney damage. This is typically achieved by addressing the root cause of the condition. Without intervention, chronic kidney disease can evolve into end-stage renal failure, a life-threatening condition that requires dialysis or a kidney transplant for survival.

Symptoms

The signs and symptoms associated with chronic kidney disease tend to emerge gradually as the condition worsens over time. These may include:

  • Experiencing nausea and vomiting
  • A reduced appetite
  • Feelings of fatigue and weakness, along with trouble sleeping
  • Alterations in urination frequency
  • Challenges in managing high blood pressure (hypertension)
  • A decline in cognitive sharpness
  • Occurrence of muscle spasms and cramps
  • Breathing difficulties due to fluid accumulation in the lungs
  • Chest discomfort if fluid surrounds the heart

  • Amount of Blood Glucose Random in mgs/dl

  • Amount of Serum Creatinine,in mgs/dl
  • Amount of Hemoglobin in gms
  • Pus Cell clumps: present,notpresent
  • Bacteria: present,notpresent
  • Hypertension: yes,no
  • Diabetes Mellitus: yes,no
  • Coronary Artery Disease: yes,no
  • Appetite: good,poor
  • Pedal Edema:yes,no
